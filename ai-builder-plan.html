What I'm building
Six automations. One goal.
Sorted by impact: hours reclaimed × direct alignment with the $2–3M target.
#1 Impact
A vetted list of 10 flatbed-ready prospects lands in my inbox every Monday morning — no hunting required.
What's happening today
Lead research is manual and invisible. No prospect lists in email, no research sessions on calendar. The hunting isn't happening at the volume needed for a $2–3M quarter.
What the automated version looks like
Scheduled agent runs Sunday night: searches east coast manufacturers and distributors in steel, lumber, and building materials. Filters by freight type, geography, and volume signals. Delivers a formatted list — company, contact, why they fit, outreach angle — to email by 7am Monday.
Medium lift ~3–4 hrs/week reclaimed Not started
#2 Impact
Every LinkedIn profile view on the JRC page gets turned into a researched contact with a draft outreach message — within 24 hours, automatically.
What's happening today
LinkedIn sends weekly summaries showing 29–33 profile views and 500+ impressions. These warm inbound signals go unactioned — no follow-up visible in email or calendar.
What the automated version looks like
Triggered when a LinkedIn notification arrives: agent extracts viewer details, researches the company, drafts a personalized connection message. Drops into Gmail as a draft ready to approve. 30 seconds to review vs. 20+ minutes of manual research.
Quick win ~2 hrs/week reclaimed Not started
#3 Impact
Follow-up with every prospect I've contacted happens on schedule without me tracking it — nothing goes cold because I forgot.
What's happening today
No follow-up tracking system visible. No structured outreach cadence on the calendar. Leads contacted once likely go silent.
What the automated version looks like
When an outreach email is sent via Gmail, a scheduled task fires: +3 days, +7 days, +14 days follow-up drafts are generated and queued. Each draft is personalized with a new angle. Approve or skip in one click.
Medium lift ~2–3 hrs/week reclaimed Not started
#4 Impact
My daily Google Alert on "Flatbed Owner Operator" gets turned into a 3-bullet industry brief with one specific action I can take — not just a list of articles.
What's happening today
Google Alert arrives daily. Raw article links, no synthesis. Extracting actionable intel is a manual 15–30 min process that likely doesn't happen consistently.
What the automated version looks like
Triggered on alert receipt: agent reads article links, summarizes key industry signals, flags any company names matching ICP, and surfaces one "do this today" action. Delivered as a 5-line digest reply in Gmail.
Quick win ~1 hr/week + intel quality boost Not started
#5 Impact
My inbox shows only things that need me — everything else is auto-sorted before I open Gmail each morning.
What's happening today
Gmail is dominated by Zillow alerts (10+/week), marketing emails from Under Armour, TaylorMade, Harbor Freight, and newsletters. Every open is a visual scan of 90% noise.
What the automated version looks like
Scheduled morning triage: agent scans inbox, labels and archives noise, flags anything from JRC domains or known contacts, and sends a "your inbox in 60 seconds" summary to the top of the inbox.
Quick win ~30 min/day reclaimed Not started
#6 Impact
Existing accounts like Home Depot get a proactive rate or lane check-in from me every 30 days — without me remembering to do it.
What's happening today
Account expansion is ops-driven, not sales-driven. The HD rate thread shows Blaise was CC'd, not originating. Relationship-building is happening through operations.
What the automated version looks like
Monthly scheduled task: for each named account, agent drafts a brief relationship email — lane volume update, market rate color, new capacity availability. Queued as a draft to personalize and send. Keeps JRC top of mind between freight cycles.
Quick win ~1–2 hrs/month + relationship compounding Not started
